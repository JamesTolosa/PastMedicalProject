First aid for someone having a severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)









                            1.The baby or child has a raised temperature (above 37°C). They have

                               hot flushed skin and may be sweating.


                                     In the early stages of fever, a baby or child may have a raised

                                     temperature but complain they feel cold and they may look pale. They

                                    may also complain of a headache.






                            2. Check their temperature by using a thermometer.


                                     A moderate fever may not be harmful, but you should monitor them. A

                                     temperature above 39°C can be dangerous.




                            3.Reduce their temperature: remove excess clothes and give them

                            fluids such as water or diluted juice.

                                     This will cool the baby or child and help to keep them hydrated. Do not

                                     cover the baby or child with blankets or anything that may make them

                                     hotter.






                            4.Give the recommended dose of paracetamol syrup.

                                     This will help to reduce the baby or child’s temperature and pain.
